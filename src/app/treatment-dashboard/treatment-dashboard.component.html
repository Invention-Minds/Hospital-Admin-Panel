How to read this board

The big number on each patient is their NEWS2 score (National Early Warning Score) — a 0–20 total calculated from 7 vital signs: respiration rate, SpO₂, supplemental oxygen, temperature, systolic BP, pulse and consciousness level. The higher the score, the sicker the patient. Patients are listed sickest-first.

0–4
STABLELow risk — routine monitoring
!
WATCHOne vital sign severely abnormal — clinician review
5–6
MEDIUMUrgent review by the ward doctor
7+
HIGHEmergency response — continuous monitoring

The mini-graph is the score trend over recent readings. Chips flag other concerns (critical lab result, no progress note today, vitals overdue, score rising 3 readings running).
